Thailand

Bangkok

"Same same, but different", this Thailish philosophy sums up Bangkok. With so much daily life conducted on the street, there're few cities in the world that reward exploration handsomely as Bangkok. It's never prevented anybody from getting on with Thai people.

Chiang Mai

Thailand's northern capital is an escape from the whirlwind pace of its southern rival life. Despite the constant arrival of sightseers, this is a place to relax after the chaos of Bangkok and recharge your batteries with fabulous food and leisurely wandering.

Ko Samui

Ko Samui is like a well-established Hollywood celebrity: she's outrageously manicured, has lovely blonde tresses. She's been in the tourism business longer than almost any other Thai island, and she's embraced a new generation of resort goers.

Phuket Town

Phuket was an island of rubber trees, tin mines and cash-hungry merchants. Today the city is testament to the island’s historical soul. Wander down streets clogged with Sino-Portuguese architecture housing arty coffee shops, and eccentric galleries.

Krabi Town

Bustling Krabi Town is majestically situated among angular limestone karsts jutting from the mangroves, but mid-city you’re more likely to be awe-struck by the sheer volume of guesthouses. It's a transport hub and a busy traveller scene continues to evolve.

Hat Patong

Sun-scorched Russians in bad knock-off T-shirts, an overwhelming disregard for managed development and a knack for turning the midlife crisis into a full-scale industry make Patong rampant with unintentional comedy.
