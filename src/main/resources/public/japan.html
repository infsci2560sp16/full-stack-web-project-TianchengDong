Japan

Tokyo

Tokyo is always on the move! Such is the joy of living in a city that prides itself on constant renewal and reinvention; it seriously never gets old. Tokyo has everything you can ask of a city, and has it in spades: a rich, cosmopolitan dining scene.

Kyoto

Kyoto is old Japan writ large: quiet temples, sublime gardens, colourful shrines and geisha scurrying to secret liaisons. You’ll find true masterpieces of religious architecture, and enjoy the entire spectrum of Japanese food, from kaiseki to rāmen.

Osaka

Osaka is Japan's third largest city, which is famous for good food. Sightseeing highlights include Osaka Castle, the Blade Runner–style nighttime scenery of the Dōtombori area, the gloriously gaudy retro storefronts of the Shin-Sekai neighborhood.

Sapporo

Japan's fifth-largest city, and the prefectural capital of Hokkaidō, Sapporo is a surprisingly dynamic and cosmopolitan urban centre that pulses with energy. Sapporo is shaped by its wide grid of tree-lined streets and ample public parks.

Nara

Japan's first permanent capital, Nara is one of the country's most rewarding destinations. The centrepiece is the Daibutsu (Great Buddha), which rivals Mt Fuji and Kyoto's Golden Pavilion (Kinkaku-ji) as Japan's single most impressive sight.

Yokohama

Yokohama has an appealing flavour and history all its own, even though it's just 20 minutes' train ride south of central Tokyo. For visitors it's the breezy bay front, creative arts scene, multiple microbreweries, jazz clubs and great international dining.
