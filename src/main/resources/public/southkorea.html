South Korea

Seoul

The capital of South Korea, and thelargest metropolis. Whatever you want, at any time of day or night, Seoul can provide. Also, you must join a guided tour to look around World Heritage–listed Changdeokgung, the most beautiful of Seoul's four main palaces.

Jeju-si

Chances are that you’ll end up spending a day or two in Jeju-do's capital, Jeju-si, as this is the main entry point to to the island, either by land or air. The city centre has a few historic structures, plenty of shopping and lively bars opposite the old City Hall.

Gwangju

Gwangju is Korea's sixth-largest city. Its history sets it apart: in 1980 a peaceful pro-democracy demonstration, the May 18 Democratic Uprising, was put down by the then military government and remains a strong part of the identity here.

Daegu

South Korea’s fourth largest city is a pleasant and progressive place with a fascinating traditional-medicine market, some excellent eating options and a humming downtown that’s good fun to explore. The museums and university are worth a visit.

Incheon

South Korea's third largest city, this expanding metropolis and industrial port – 36km west of Seoul – is the place where Korea opened up to the world in 1883. Come here to stroll along the Wolmido waterfront and visit the fish market at Yeonan.

Jeonju

Jeonju, the provincial capital of Jeollabuk-do, is famous for being the birthplace of both the Joseon dynasty and Korea’s most well-known culinary delight, bibimbap. The city is the perfect base from which to explore Jeollabuk-do.
